# JLPT N5 — Complete 30-Day Study Package
**Grammar-led · Date-agnostic · Go at your own pace**

---

## The philosophy: grammar-led learning

Each day works in this order:
1. **Grammar first** — learn 2–3 patterns (the structure)
2. **Vocab second** — the day's words are chosen to *fill* those patterns
3. **Kanji throughout** — 3–4 new kanji/day, mnemonic-based

This is how good tutors teach: you learn は + です, then immediately learn the nouns that let you build "X は Y です" sentences. You never learn a word with no pattern to use it in, and you never learn a pattern with no words to fill it. Every day you can produce real sentences.

This beats pure thematic blocks (memorizing 40 food words you can't use in a sentence yet) and pure frequency lists (no structure to hang words on).

---

## Why this order is optimal

| Phase | Days | Focus | Why here |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation | 1–6 | は/が/を/に/で particles + です | The skeleton. Everything plugs into these. |
| Verbs core | 7–13 | ます/て/ない/た forms + verb groups | Verbs are the engine. て-form especially unlocks dozens of later patterns. |
| Adjectives | 14–18 | い/な-adjective conjugation | Description. Builds on noun sentences from phase 1. |
| Expression | 19–25 | たい, あげる/もらう, permission, obligation | Real communication. Requires verbs (phase 2) as a base. |
| Consolidation | 26–30 | Counters, conjunctions, review, mock exams | Tie it together, drill gaps, test readiness. |

Grammar is introduced so that each new pattern depends only on what came before — never on something you haven't learned yet. The て-form (Day 10) comes before all the patterns that need it (てください, ている, てから, てもいい). Verbs come before たい (want to do a verb). Adjectives come before adjective-based expressions.

## Daily routine (4+ hrs)

| Block | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Read the day's grammar patterns. Write 3 sentences per pattern by hand. | 45 min |
| 2 | Learn the day's vocab. Read each example sentence aloud, visualize it. Add to Anki. | 45 min |
| 3 | Learn the day's kanji. Get/make a mnemonic per kanji. Add to Anki. | 45 min |
| 4 | Anki reviews (all due cards, both decks). | 60 min |
| 5 | Listening immersion — any native content. | 30 min |

> Anki review load grows over time. By day 20 expect 90+ min of reviews. That's the system working, not a problem.

## Checkpoints (by day, not date)

### After Day 6 — Foundation check
- Can you build "X は Y です" / "X が Y を V します" sentences?
- Particles は/が/を/に/で/へ/の/も used correctly?
- ~25 kanji, ~170 vocab

### After Day 13 — Verb check
- Conjugate any verb into ます/ません/ました/て/ない/た?
- Know which group (1/2/3) a verb belongs to?
- て-form solid? (This is the make-or-break skill.)
- ~50 kanji, ~370 vocab

### After Day 18 — Adjective check
- Conjugate い and な adjectives (present/neg/past/past-neg)?
- ~70 kanji, ~520 vocab
- Take a free N5 vocab/grammar section at jlptsensei.com — target 55%

### After Day 25 — Grammar complete
- All 60 grammar points seen and practiced?
- ~95 kanji, ~720 vocab
- Full N5 mock — target 55%+ overall

### After Day 30 — Exam ready
- All 103 kanji, ~800 vocab, 60 grammar points
- Full timed mock — target 65%+
- 70%+ → start N4. 55–65% → one more review week. <55% → drill weak section.
